Abouts Us

North American Sikh Review was started way back in the year 2006 by a group of Sikhs, headed by Sardar Avtaar Singh, who were highly concerned about the survival of their mother tongue Punjabi. At that time their was a report from UNESCO regarding the lost languages of the modern world; that the Punjabi language will not survive more than fifty years. Keeping in mind the current situation in India and broad, this report was proving correct. Remember, if we failed to educate our children and the coming generations regarding our great language and heritage,Sikh’s achievements, Sikhism and its great history and the historical facts than this report will automatically proves its truth. On the other side, if we kept them informed with the history and the latest of our Punjabi and Gurmukhi than UNESCO’s report will prove to be incorrect. Hence, this magazine was introduced to keep live our great language and the Sikh’s heritage and their achievements.

Our Mission

Our basic aim was to educate our Sikh families and coming generations in America and Canada regarding Sikhism, Great Sikhs and their achievements, our great history and culture. In our earlier additions, we were successful in providing that unique material which was neither available in any magazine or newspaper but was not printed in books. We had received numerous letters and telephonic calls confirming and admiring our efforts and the unique aim. This had given our readers both the spiritual bliss as well as the education. They were able to move more close to our Gurus and Punjab, their history and culture. Thanks to the great AKALPURKH, we were able to serve you with the best.
